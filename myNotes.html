Despite concerns of rapid slowdowns caused by restructuring in China and continued issues with the global economy, our July survey indicates that the constructing equipment industry remains healthy across a number of categories. Pricing was relatively stable in China and Brazil, while we saw decline in India caused by overstocking in Front-loaders and Backhoes. We expect this situation to be temporary and remain bullish on pricing for India in general. Demand remained strong across most markets, with Brazil experience strength based on the Olympics and other infrastructure projects driven by government initiatives. Demand in China is expected to slack based on reforms, but we have not yet seen the effects of these reforms coming down the pipeline, indicating potential earnings surprises, especially for MTW and CAT. Inventory remains in-line with the current climate, and we do not see indications that we are facing a sever glut of equipment in cranes, backhoes, or front-loaders. The key take-aways are for earning to be in-line with consensus for Terex, while there is a case for an earnings surprise for both CAT and MTW. Using consensus estimates for N. America and Europe, and modeling in unexpected strength in Emerging markets based on our surveys, we see potential for an additional $0.05 in EPS for CAT and $0.02 for MTW (See models for additional details).
